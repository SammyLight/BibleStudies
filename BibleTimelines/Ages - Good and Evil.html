All Men are by Creation Gods Children/People (through Christ)

  1. By creation, all men are under Pre-Incarnate Christ
    1. The One through whom God created man and the world,
    2. Gods Right Hand and Representative,
    3. The Radiance of Gods Glory,
    4. The Exact Representation of Gods Being,
    5. The Judge of All the Earth. 
  2. By creation, man did not have
  3. Eternal life,
    1. The Nature & Glory of God (he was created mortal, of the dust of the ground), and
    2. Access into Heaven, the Holy of Holies, the very Presence of God, (the Garden of Eden
      is equivalent to the Holy Place and not to the Holy of Holies).
  4. By his transgression
    1. Man and Creation come under condemnation (the curse of death)the Devils power,
    2. Man is driven out of the Garden of Eden/Holy Place into Eden/the Courtyard
      (He is driven out of the Garden because of the blessing there which he had become
      disqualified to possess. Nevertheless, man is still in Gods presence and still under Christ).
    3. Re-entering the Garden was made impossible by the Cherubim guarding the entrance to the
      Garden and the flaming sword guarding the way to the Tree of Life.
  5. Man needs
    1. Eternal life (and freedom from condemnation)  to re-enter the Holy Place, and
    2. The Glory of God to enter the Most Holy Place (man was created for glory)

Eden Temple DestroyedMan is Cast out of Gods Presence

  1. Eden (and its Garden) was Gods first dwelling place on earth. This temple was for all mankind.
    1. There was no Jew/Gentile distinction
    2. There was however a Seth/Cain distinction
  2. The destruction of the Temple of Eden was for mankind as the destruction of the Temple of Solomon was for Israel; it meant that
    1. Men were no longer Gods people, God had cast them off (God was no longer dwelling among men),
    2. Men were no longer under his blessing and protection,
  3. The destruction of Eden did not mean that
    1. God would absolutely have nothing to do with men ever again (he spoke to Daniel, Ezekiel, etc., even after the destruction of Jerusalem and the temple and performed miracles for them, even in their exile. And though he rejected Israel, he had a remnant among them.)

Men come under the Ps & Ps

  1. Men are removed from under the direct authority of Christ, and thus, from under God and are no longer His people
  2. Men are given their inheritance but not having glory, they must be placed under Gs & Ts. Thus,
  3. They are divided among Christ and the other lower sons of God. (God/Christ chooses Israel as his people)
  4. The Gs & Ts are all under God, and so everyone still belongs to God. It matters however, through whom one belongs to God.
    1. Just like the mother of Abrahams sons, Ishmael and Isaac, mattered
  5. All future benefits, such as provision for eternal life and glorification become unavailable to the Gentiles, because they are no longer under Christ and have become disconnected from him

Abrahams Descendants are all Gods People/Children by Covenant

  1. Christ, being Gods representative on earth,
    choses Abraham and his descendants through Isaac
    and Jacob and becomes their Lord
  2. God makes covenant with Abraham making him
    and his chosen descendants, Gods own people
  3. Even before the Old Covenant, God called Israel, My
    People. This shows that it is not the Law, but the
    Abrahamic Covenant that made Israel Gods people
  4. Canaan land is Abrahams allotted inheritance

Israel is by Covenant Gods People/Children

  1. Israel was put under God and Christs holy angels (not Satan and his angels)
  2. Through these holy angels, Israel is indeed Gods people (and under Christ) as Abraham was promised
  3. These holy angels are like Hagar and Christ like Sarah
  4. The Law offered Israel Eternal Life & inheritance

All nations are Gods People/ Children by the New Covenant

  1. All nations are once again under Christ
    1. Christ destroys death and the Ps & Ps
    2. His glorified servants are kings in all the earth and through them owns all the nations
    3. And they all come under the covenant he is under with God

Christs disciples are all Gods children

  1. Christ abolishes the flesh of all his, thus,
    1. Fulfilling the circumcision requirement of the Abrahamic Covenant for all in him
    2. Destroying the bodily distinction between races and sexes
    3. Destroying the claim of the Ps & Ps on Gentiles and
    4. Creating a new nation
    5. making it possible for Gentiles to join Abrahams family
  2. Christ abolishes the Law
  3. Christ ratifies a New Covenant, making all who are his Sons of God

Mortality

God created us to rule the creation and for this we need the divine glory of God. We were however made a little lower than angels, we did not have the required glory, i.e., body type to fulfil our calling.

Apart from the purpose of God for creating us, we needed eternal life and also, 

Divine Purpose

To fulfil the purpose for which we were created, i.e., to rule creation for God, we must be glorified. Therefore, anything that stands against our coming to glory is a serious problem.

Disqualification for Life

Because of the condemnation Adam was under, he was disqualified from gaining eternal life.

God plainly illustrated this consequence of Adam's transgression and condemnation when he drove Adam out of the Garden of Eden so that he would not eat of the Tree of Life and live forever [Gen 3:22-23,24].

So, because Adam was under condemnation,

  1. God drove Adam out of the Garden of Eden so as not to eat of the Tree of Life
  2. God put Cherubim on the east side of the Garden to prevent Adam or any of his descendants from entrance into the Garden
  3. God put a flaming sword, flashing back and forth to guard the way to the Tree of Life. Therefore, not only man, but even angels could not get the fruit from the Tree of Life for man.

This is a problem because if we cannot have eternal life, then we cannot be glorified. Glory cannot be given to those who are under condemnation, who are not to have eternal life.

Banishement From Heaven As Well

The reason why Adam and Eve could not enter the Garden of Eden was becasue of the Tree of Life in it. Since Adam had been condemned by God, God could not allow him eat of the Tree of Life and live forever. But, if the Tree wont be cut down for Adams sake, the only way to prevent this from happening was to banish Adam from the Garden of Eden.

The Tree which was the reason for the banishment of Adam and his wife was not only present in the Garden of Eden but is also present in heaven. Therefore, just as he could not be allowed access into the Garden of Eden, so too, he could not be allowed access to heaven. Most certainly, man could not enter that part of heaven where the Tree of Life was present.

Therefore, the disqualification of Adam from gaining eternal life not only meant that he could not enter the Garden of Eden again but it also meant that he could not enter heaven.

Sinfulness

Adam coming under the curse of corruption also creates a major problem, which is sinfulness.

One of the major manifestations of corruption in the flesh is sin--the natural inclination of men to do evil.

Sin as a problem is mostly realized under the Law, where it is transgression resulting in fresh condemnation. Nevertheless, even before the Law, man's inclination to wrong was what brought on the flood.

Sin is a problem because, one way or another, it will lead to disfavour from God and even to condemnation.

The Withdrawal of the Spirit

God said that His Spirit would not contend with man forever and gave men 120 years until the flood [Gen 6:3-4 KJV].

In this judgment, God specified that man was "flesh". This means that the judgment is attached to the flesh of man. That is, as long as man is flesh, the Spirit will not contend with him after the stated time.

Through prophet Joel, God promised to reverse this judgment

In the last days, I will pour out my Spirit on all flesh. Your sons and daughters will prophesy, your old men will dream dreams, your young men will see visions. 29Even on my servants, both men and women, I will pour out my Spirit in those days. [Joe 2:28-29]

Joels prophecy means that the Spirit had been withdrawn at some earlier time.

The Loss of Gods First Templethe Temple of Eden

The flood covered the whole earth and destroyed everything. Therefore, since the Eden and its Garden were on earth, the flood destroyed Eden.

Since Eden was the first temple of God on earth, the flood therefore destroyed God's first temple and left the earth and the remnant of men without a temple.

This has serious implications, it means that

  1. God is not dwelling on earth with us,
  2. Men are no longer recognized as Gods people
  3. Men have no direct access to God's presence, thus we must go through intermediaries who do...
  4. Men are no longer under the direct protection and oversight of God
  5. The temple is a sign that we are Gods people. If we dont have access to Gods temple, then we are NOT REALLY HIS PEOPLE.
  6. And if we are not Gods people, we cannot be glorified!

This situation was partially restored under the Law but it was quite limited and only the Jews had access to the Tabernacle/Temple.

Living in Exile

The destruction of Eden and man being cut off from Eden the temple of God is akin to Israel and Judahs exile from the Land of Israel accompanied with the destruction of the Temple.

Therefore, since the flood, men have been living in a form of exile from the presence of God.

Blessings After the Flood

Gen 9

  1. The Rainbow Covenant ensured that
    1. God would never again destroy the earth with a flood
    2. God would never again destroy the earth living so few persons alive
  2. The right to eat meat as food
  3. The fear and dread of us coming on the animals is a fulfilment of the mandate to rule over everything that moves on the ground [Gen 1:28]

The Priesthood of Shem

By calling Yahweh the God of Shem, Noah made Shem the spiritual head of his family, he made him the priest of the mankind.

Because of this blessing, those who are to be priests of God must emerge from Shem's family.

The Supremacy of Shem

Also, by stating that Japheth would dwell in the tents of Shem and that Canaan would be their slave, Shem had been made the ruler of mankind.

In other words, the ruler of the earth will be from the line of Shem. It doesn't matter who ruled during whatever period, in the end, the world will be under a Shemite.

Therefore, those who will rule must come from the line of Shem. If they are not natural descendants, then they must be legitimately adopted into the line of Shem.

Israel having no king over them was presented as a problem [Jdg 21:25]. In the same way, human kind not having a ruler is a problem [Hab 1:14]. Therefore, human beings are to have a Ruler over them and whoever this will be, he must come from the line of Shem.

The Loss of Eden and the Presence of God

Eden was God's first dwelling, his first Temple Complex on earth after the creation. Its presence on earth meant that God was with us. This was man's first place of physical worship and approach to God. Even though they were outside of the Garden of Eden, Cain still referred to Eden as the Presence of God.

The flood however brought about an end to Eden, apparently, as with many other things at the time, it was destroyed in the flood.

The destruction of Eden meant that God is no longer physically dwelling among men.

The Division of the Earth to the Sons of Adam

When the Most High gave the nations their inheritance, when he divided the sons of Adam, he set up boundaries for the peoples according to the number of the sons of God. 9For Yahwehs portion is his people, Jacob his allotted inheritance. [Deut 32:8-9]

From these words of Moses we understand that when God confused the language of men at Babel, he also divided the earth to them [Gen 11:8-9; Act 17:26-27].

By referring to men as the sons of Adam, what Moses has in mind is the authority Adam was given over the earth [Gen 1:26,28]. In other words, God divided the earth to men as the sons of Adam. It is as father dividing his property to his sons.

Therefore, the different branches of Adams family have been given their share of the land in Adam.

Men Under Principalities and Powers

Moses also points out that God appointed sons of God, i.e., angels, over the nations and their portion of the earth.

The Problem Arising From Putting Men Under the Sons of God

Putting men under the sons of God...

  1. When the prodigal son was given his inheritance, he left his fathers house. When sons are given their inheritance they leave home; it means that they are no longer under their fathers direct authority but have been given independence.
    Therefore, giving men their inheritance means that God as a father has given men their independence from him. This means that men are no longer directly under his care.
  2. Setting the sons of God over men means that men are no longer directly under God.

This does not stop men from being God's children, however, it becomes a serious problem under the Law. The Law recognizes the other nations as being under other gods...

The Abrahamic Covenant and the Alienation of Men

This covenant affected everything.

  • While this covenant did not change the fact that anyone could worship God, it, however, ensured that only Abraham and his children could inherit the land of Canaan and thus be God's people.
    • In other words, this covenant ensured that, except for Abraham and his children, no other people could be Gods people. People could serve God, but they would not be recognized as His people except they were Abrahams children through the chosen line (of Isaac and Jacob).
  • This covenant effectively put an end to the Edenic order of things.
    • Since Noah and his sons were citizens of Eden, though Eden had been destroyed in the flood, people could still be thought of as Gods people. When the Israelites were exiled from the land of Israel, they were still Gods people. By choosing a New Eden, so to speak, and making only Abraham and his children citizens of this New Eden, God by this covenant had effectively cut off all other peoples.
  • This covenant making Abraham and his children Gods people, however, took full effect when they took possession of the land.
    • Therefore, it is the institution of the Law that really changed everything. The Law, which gave Abrahams children the Tabernacle of God and by which they took possession of the land, made it impossible for others to be Gods people except for the Israelites.
    • The Law, through the Abrahamic covenant put an end to Eden.

EdenGods First Temple

Eden was a temple complex and thus was the first temple of God on earth.

A king lives in his palace among his people. For a king to not live among his people means that they are not really his people.

A temple is the palace or house of a God. A temple on earth means that God is our King and he is living among us. It means that we are favoured.

The Principalities and Powers

These came to power at Babel when God divided the earth to the sons of Adam.

Apparently, when the earth was divided to the sons of Adam, it was also divided to the sons of God.

The right of the sons of Adam to the earth, why it was divided to them is the right of Adam to the earth. It is consistent with Gods blessing upon Adam in the beginning that he should multiply and subdue the earth.

God blessed them and said to them, Be fruitful and increase in number; fill the earth and subdue it. Rule over the fish of the sea and the birds of the air and over every living creature that moves on the ground. 29Then God said, I give you every seed bearing plant on the face of the whole earth and every tree that has fruit with seed in it. They will be yours for food. [Gen 1:28-29]

What was the source of the legitimacy of the sons of God being over the sons of Adam and the earth?

Is it the curse of corruption?

Is it the withdrawal of Gods Spirit?

The creation came under the power of corruption after Adam transgressed and was judged. Effectively, it came under the power of Satan. Therefore, the earth was under two powers at the same time.

Therefore, since they were also over the earth, if the earth was divided, then it would not be divided to sons of Adam alone. Rather, it could also have been divided to the sons of God if they were under Satan as administrators of the curse of corruption.

It seems, however, that the authority of the principalities and powers is over the earth through men. That is, the ones they have direct power over are the sons of Adam and not the earth itself.
